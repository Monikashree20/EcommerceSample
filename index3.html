Blackberries belong to the genus Rubus in the rose family (Rosaceae).They are usually found in north temperate regions.Wild blackberries thrive in eastern North America and along the Pacific coast.These prickly fruit-bearing bushes produce dark, edible fruits. Blackberry plants have biennial canes (stems) covered with prickles. The leaves typically feature three or five oval, coarsely toothed leaflets. The flowers can be white, pink, or red, and they give rise to black or red-purple fruits.
